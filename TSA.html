We are Chapter 2111, and we currently have about twenty members. Our chapter has been slowly growing over the years, and we have members competing in various events this year. Our History
Mckinney Boyd's Computer Science Club was founded in 2015 by Thai Nguyen and Sammy Shin, who sought to help others learn how to program. The Engineering Club has been a part of student life at Boyd since 2010, and they worked together with both TSA and the Living Roof. Now, the clubs joined to help everyone work on their TSA projects, meet new people they can partner with, and just see other available options they can try next time.
Community Service Projects
We volunteer at Hour of Code events in schools within our community. This is an oppportunity where we provide lessons to those interested in computer science who don't have the resources to get started. We also volunteer in the Eddins Elementary Coding Club, where we teach beginner lessons in programming to children from third to fifth grade, while helping to tutor kids in other TSA-related subjects, from fashion design to debate to architecture to building 3-D models.
Other Activities
Our club has joined in on many different projects to help improve our community. One of them is the Living Roof project, where the goal is to help preserve our environment. This project plans a roof that is at least partially covered with shrubs, bushes, and even small trees, all of which are planted over a waterproof surface. It can also include other layers such as a root barrier and drainage and irrigation systems to help keep the roof functioning. It would be placed above the school roof, and would provide lots of benefits to the students.

Our chapter's young age means we do not yet have many awards to our name. However, we have won a variety in the past, including such as the 2017 National Coding Title. Of course, this year, we hope to increase our school's collection of awards, and have set our sights on success, especially in webmaster. All of our teams are working hard to succeed this year, and with the effort everyone are putting into their various projects, they will.
Our Creed
We believe that our education, especially in technological fields, is critical for our life and our future. We believe there is always a need to improve our attitude, work ethic, skills, and the knowledge we hold. We are guided by our teachers, other experts we trust, and our own beliefs; we will strive to do our best in improving both our community and the world at large.
We accept the responsibilities that is placed upon our shoulders. We accept the moral codes that have been developed by others in the past. We will explore for safer, more effective methods of working and living. We will develop beneficial attitudes and exercise tact and respect for other individuals. Through manual and intellectual work, we will express our ideas to the best of our abilities to the society around us. We will make it our goal to be and do better each day, and to be steadfast in our belief in ourself, and our fellow Americans.
